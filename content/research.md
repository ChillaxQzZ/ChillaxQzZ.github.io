3D medical imaging reveals the complexity of human anatomy and pathological structures with unprecedented accuracy, and cutting-edge artificial intelligence models are reshaping the way we extract diagnostic criteria from massive visual information. However, how to make these large models cross the dimensional gap and efficiently and accurately understand the complex three-dimensional spatial structure remains a fundamental challenge. My research work is at the intersection of biomedical engineering and artificial intelligence, utilizing spatial algorithms and dimensionality reduction reconstruction techniques to explore the two-dimensional visualization representation of complex medical data.

## Current Directions

### Spatial modeling and feature extraction of cardiovascular pathology

I use advanced geometric calculation strategies such as Symbolic Distance Function (SDF) to integrate complex cardiovascular 3D imaging data. Through precise field modeling, I am committed to tracking deep clues hidden in complex vascular topologies and achieving precise anchoring and dissection of key lesion areas such as thrombosis and blood stasis.

### Medical Visual Data Adaptation for Large Models

I develop a computational process dedicated to crossing dimensions - covering 3D surface reconstruction and 2D projection optimization mapping - specifically designed to convert high-dimensional spatial pathological information into 2D feature maps that are easy to parse by large language and visual models, without loss and clarity. I am also exploring the construction of efficient data processing pipelines to automate the workflow of complex medical image dimensionality reduction.
